## Section 1 — Getting to Work: Transport Update

This morning your commute deserves extra attention.

The Rail, Maritime and Transport Workers union tube strike, which has been causing significant disruption on the London Underground this week, is officially ending today. However, Transport for London is warning that **knock-on disruption should be expected this morning** as services gradually get back up and running. Trains will be limited before 6:30am, and services were advised to finish before 9pm during the action.

During the strike, the **Central line had no trains running between White City and Liverpool Street**. South Woodford sits on the eastern branch of the Central line, and while that specific suspension covered a different stretch, the knock-on effect across the whole line means delays and crowding are likely — especially at interchange stations like Stratford.

**Today's recommendation: stick to your buses.** Take the **W13 from South Woodford to Wanstead**, then the **101 to East Ham**. No specific service disruptions were reported on the W13 or 101 routes. Buses ran normally throughout the strike period as a Transport for London-recommended alternative.

If you are tempted to take the train, be prepared for residual delays and packed platforms. The bus route is the more reliable choice this morning.

**Today is Friday, not Tuesday, so no train reminder today** — but a note for next week: if there are no service disruptions on Tuesday, the train is the recommended option.
