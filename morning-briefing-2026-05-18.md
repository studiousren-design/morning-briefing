## Section 4: Broader US Market — Key Themes This Week

### The Iran Conflict Is the Dominant Macro Theme
The US-Iran conflict, which began on 28 February 2026, has driven **oil prices up more than 50%**. Brent crude reached **$111.23 per barrel**, with Iran's closure of the Strait of Hormuz disrupting approximately 20% of global oil supply — described by the International Energy Agency as the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market. This is stoking inflation fears and raising the risk of stagflation. The Dow Jones fell **537 points (–1.07%) on Friday**, closing at 49,526, directly weighed down by these concerns.

### Treasury Yields at a One-Year High
The 10-year US Treasury yield jumped **9 basis points to 4.55%** — the highest level in a year. This creates headwinds for growth stocks and risk assets, including technology names and Bitcoin.

### Federal Reserve — No Rate Cuts in Sight
The Federal Reserve held rates steady at **3.5–3.75%** for a third consecutive meeting. Chair Powell made clear there is no urgency to cut rates; policy remains data-dependent. **FOMC minutes from the April meeting will be released on Wednesday, 20 May** — the same day as NVIDIA's earnings, making Wednesday a double-catalyst day. J.P. Morgan Research expects the Fed to hold through all of 2026, with a possible 25 basis point rate *hike* in the third quarter of 2027.

### US Stock Futures Edging Lower This Morning
Markets opened cautiously, with futures edging lower as investors await Wednesday's NVIDIA earnings report for fresh signals on the durability of the artificial intelligence-driven rally.
