## 5. Broader United States Stock Market Events

### University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment — Final May Reading (Due Today)
The final May reading of the University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment survey is expected to be released today. The preliminary reading earlier in May came in at 48.2 — a record low since tracking began in 1952. This was driven by:

- Surging petrol prices (cited by one-third of consumers)
- Tariff concerns (cited by nearly 30% of consumers)
- Year-ahead inflation expectations remaining elevated at 4.5%

Watch for whether the final reading comes in better or worse than the preliminary. A further decline could pressure equity markets, particularly consumer discretionary and retail stocks.

### Earnings Season Wrap-Up
The first-quarter 2026 earnings season is drawing to a close, with strong aggregate results:
- 84% of Standard and Poor's 500 companies reported a positive earnings per share surprise

This is a broadly positive backdrop for equities heading into the summer.
