## Section 3: Stock Watch — Your Portfolio

Let's go through the tickers that matter to you, grouped by theme.

### Artificial Intelligence and Semiconductors

**NVIDIA (NVDA)** is trading around **$215**, having moved between $212.89 and $217.80 on Friday. No major new catalyst today, but the stock remains a core AI infrastructure name with over 1,300% growth over five years. Nvidia has already committed $40 billion to equity AI deals so far this year.

**Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSM)** reported exceptional first quarter 2026 results, with revenue of **$35.9 billion — up 40.6% year-over-year** — beating consensus estimates. Their next earnings report is expected in July. Momentum remains strong, driven entirely by artificial intelligence chip demand.

**Qualcomm (QCOM)** — no major standalone news today. The stock is part of the broader semiconductor rally and benefits from the same AI tailwinds. Worth watching as the week's inflation data could reshape rate expectations and affect growth stocks.

**iShares Global Technology Exchange-Traded Fund (IXN)** last closed around **$132.57**, with a session range of $128.85 to $132.57 on Friday. This exchange-traded fund tracks the S&P Global Information Technology Sector Index and is influenced by many of the same names across your portfolio.

### Big Tech and Platforms

**Alphabet (GOOG)** — no specific standalone news today. The market is broadly watching inflation data on Tuesday, which will drive rate sentiment across all mega-cap technology stocks.

**Meta (META)** posted **$56.3 billion in revenue for the first quarter of 2026**, up 33% year-over-year. Capital expenditure hit a record $72.2 billion in 2025, and management is projecting it could reach as high as **$145 billion in 2026** as the company pours money into AI infrastructure. Artificial intelligence is central to Meta's advertising and product roadmap.

**Apple (AAPL)** — no major standalone events today. Apple is broadly positioned as a potential beneficiary of the on-device AI wave, and analysts are watching for any product announcements tied to their next developer conference cycle.

**Palantir Technologies (PLTR)** — implied volatility for near-term options has been elevated, with the May weekly implied volatility at 87 and monthly at 73, against a 52-week range of 41 to 86. This suggests the market is pricing in significant movement potential — worth monitoring if you hold options positions.

### Crypto and Digital Assets

**Bitcoin (BTC-USD)** is trading at approximately **$81,503**. A key narrative driving this is what analysts are calling a Bitcoin supply crunch — exchange-traded funds are absorbing new mining output faster than it can be replaced, which contributed to breaking through the $80,000 resistance level.

**iShares Bitcoin Trust Exchange-Traded Fund (IBIT)** is trading around **$45.45**. US spot Bitcoin exchange-traded funds posted five consecutive days of net inflows totalling nearly $1.7 billion through last week, with BlackRock's IBIT leading with $134.6 million in a single session. The sector is now on track for its sixth straight week of positive flows — the longest such streak since July 2025. JPMorgan notes Bitcoin is increasingly competing with gold as a debasement hedge.

**Coinbase (COIN)** reported first quarter 2026 earnings last week and **missed estimates significantly**: earnings per share came in at a loss of $1.49, against expectations of a 27-cent profit. Revenue was $1.41 billion, below the $1.52 billion consensus — a 31% year-over-year decline. The miss was driven by lower cryptocurrency trading volumes as prices were subdued during the quarter. Watch for any bounce-back commentary or analyst revisions this week.

### Gold

**iShares Gold MiniShares Trust (GLDM)** — gold exchange-traded funds are still working through outflows from the March geopolitical shock linked to the Iran conflict. Meanwhile, Bitcoin exchange-traded fund inflows have diverged sharply upward. Gold remains a safe-haven asset but is losing some of the debasement trade to Bitcoin in the short term, according to JPMorgan analysis.

### Robotics and Medical Technology

**Intuitive Surgical (ISRG)** had a strong first quarter: revenue of **$2.77 billion, up 23% year-over-year**. Operating income jumped 40% to $1.08 billion, with margins expanding from 34% to 39%. Analysts have lifted price targets above $700. Next earnings expected in July.

### Defence and Aerospace

**L3Harris Technologies (LHXBR / LHX)** — note that LHXBR appears to be associated with L3Harris Technologies bond or preferred securities. On the equity side, **LHX** reported first quarter 2026 revenue of $5.74 billion with earnings per share of $2.72, and management raised full-year earnings guidance to a range of $11.40 to $11.60 per share. Organic revenue grew an impressive **15% year-over-year**, with a book-to-bill ratio of 1.4 times, meaning they booked $7.8 billion in orders against $5.7 billion in revenue. A new missile subsidiary named Axyv has filed a confidential S-1 for an initial public offering targeting the second half of 2026. Despite this, the stock has pulled back around 15% over the past month, currently trading near $302. Some insider selling was noted last week.

### Solid-State Batteries

**QuantumScape (QS)** jumped sharply after its first quarter earnings beat — net loss narrowed to $100.8 million or $0.16 per share, better than the $0.21 loss a year earlier and ahead of the Street's $0.18 estimate. Customer billings reached $11 million, and the company holds a strong cash cushion of $904.7 million. Shares recently surged 22% on news that the company is pivoting its solid-state battery technology towards artificial intelligence data centre and defence applications beyond its automotive roots. Today the stock is trading around **$7.53**.

## Section 4: Broader US Stock Market

This is shaping up to be a pivotal week for markets.

**The S&P 500** is trading near all-time highs, having broken above **7,300** for the first time ever, extending its longest six-week winning streak since 2024. Earnings growth at the largest technology companies is still outpacing the rest of the index by more than 40%.

### Key Events This Week

**Tuesday 12 May — April Consumer Price Index (CPI) data (8:30am US Eastern time):** This is the single biggest market catalyst of the week. Consensus expects headline CPI to rise 0.6% month-over-month and 3.7% year-over-year. Core CPI is expected at 0.3% month-over-month and 2.7% year-over-year. A hotter-than-expected print could reset Federal Reserve rate cut expectations and put pressure on growth stocks across your portfolio — including NVDA, GOOG, META, AAPL, and the technology exchange-traded funds.

**Wednesday 13 May:** Cisco Systems and Alibaba report earnings. Cisco is a key proxy for enterprise networking and artificial intelligence data centre buildout spend. Alibaba's results will be closely watched for Chinese technology and cloud demand signals. Producer Price Index data also lands on Wednesday.

**Thursday 14 May:** Applied Materials reports earnings, with consensus expecting earnings per share of around $2.68, up approximately 12% year-over-year. This is a direct read on the semiconductor equipment cycle. Retail sales data also releases Thursday.

**Friday 15 May:** Industrial production and capacity utilisation data.

### Geopolitical Watch

Middle East tensions remain a background factor. President Trump rejected Iran's latest peace proposal over the weekend, pushing oil prices higher. This could feed into Tuesday's inflation print if energy prices remain elevated. Asian markets opened positively on artificial intelligence trade optimism, suggesting some resilience to the geopolitical noise, but the inflation data will be the real test.
