**Bottom line:** Your usual bus commute via W13 and 101 is running, but factor in the Route 101 diversion at East Ham. Leave a few minutes earlier than usual. Train remains a solid alternative if you prefer.

## Section 5: Other Major Events Affecting the US Stock Market

### Kevin Warsh is Now the Federal Reserve Chair — Starting Today
This is the biggest macro story of the week. **Jerome Powell's term as Federal Reserve Chair officially ended today, 15 May 2026.** The Senate confirmed Kevin Warsh as the new chair in a 54-45 vote on 13 May. Powell will remain on the Federal Reserve Board as a governor.

Warsh is widely considered more **hawkish** than Powell on inflation, and he takes the helm at a difficult moment:
- US inflation remains above the 2% target — it has been above target for over five years.
- Tariffs and a surge in oil prices from Middle East tensions are adding upward price pressure.
- As of 14 May, investors in futures markets were pricing in **less than a 3% probability of a rate cut** at any remaining Federal Open Market Committee meeting in 2026.

**Warsh's first Federal Open Market Committee meeting as chair is scheduled for 16–17 June.** His tone and any public remarks today could move bond yields, the US dollar, and interest-rate sensitive sectors including technology and real estate.

### US-China Summit in Beijing
President Trump is meeting with President Xi Jinping in Beijing this week. Agenda items include trade imbalances, the Taiwan situation, the Iran conflict, and new bilateral boards for economic and artificial intelligence oversight. This is a significant geopolitical development. Any positive signals from the summit could boost sentiment for Apple, Qualcomm, and other companies with China manufacturing or sales exposure. Negative signals — or breakdown in talks — could reverse that quickly.

### Markets at Record Highs
The S&P 500 closed at a **record high of 7,501.24** on 14 May. The Dow Jones Industrial Average crossed 50,000 for the first time, closing at 50,063.46. The Nasdaq Composite also hit a record close at 26,635.22. The market is being driven by strong tech earnings, artificial intelligence enthusiasm, and optimism around US-China trade diplomacy.

### Cisco Systems Surge
Cisco Systems surged 13.4% on Thursday after raising both revenue and earnings guidance. While not in your portfolio directly, Cisco's strength signals healthy enterprise technology spending — a positive read for PLTR, GOOG, and other enterprise-facing technology holdings.

### Economic Data Due Today: Industrial Production
April industrial production and capacity utilisation figures are scheduled for release today. These reports feed directly into the Federal Reserve's assessment of economic health and Warsh's first policy narrative. A strong reading could reinforce the case for holding rates higher for longer.
