### The Federal Reserve's June 2026 Decision — The Big Story This Week

The Federal Open Market Committee held its benchmark interest rate steady at a target range of **3.50% to 3.75%** on Tuesday the 17th of June — an unanimous 12-0 decision, and the first meeting chaired by new Fed Chair **Kevin Warsh**.

But the hold was anything but dovish. The summary of projections — the so-called "dot plot" — **flipped hawkish**: the median policymaker now expects rates to end 2026 *higher* than they are today. That is a significant shift from March, when the median projection still implied a rate cut.

Key details:
- **9 out of 18 officials** now see at least one rate hike this year; **6 officials** see at least two hikes.
- **17 of 18 officials** judged the risks to inflation as tilted to the upside.
- May 2026 inflation (Consumer Price Index) rose to **4.2% year-over-year**, partly driven by energy price spikes from geopolitical tensions.
- A strong May jobs report further removed urgency for rate cuts.

This is significant for your portfolio: higher-for-longer rates tend to weigh on growth stocks, high-multiple technology names, and speculative assets like QuantumScape. Gold (GLDM) faces short-term headwinds from a stronger dollar but is supported by inflation concerns.
