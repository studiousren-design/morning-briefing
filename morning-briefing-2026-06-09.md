## Section 3 — Your Stocks: What's Moving

Here's your rundown on each of the stocks in your portfolio, with the latest news and events that could move them today.

### NVIDIA (NVDA)
Strong momentum continues for NVIDIA. The stock was trading in the **$206 to $210.80 range as of Monday June 8**, building on its dominant position in the artificial intelligence infrastructure space.

The freshest news: NVIDIA announced a **partnership with South Korea's SK Hynix for next-generation memory co-development**. This is significant — next-generation high-bandwidth memory is a critical bottleneck for artificial intelligence accelerator performance, and locking in a development partnership with SK Hynix is a strategic move that reinforces NVIDIA's supply chain ahead of its next generation of chips.

The broader semiconductor sector had a strong Monday recovery, with the **VanEck Semiconductor exchange-traded fund gaining 5%** — though it didn't fully recover all of the previous week's losses. NVIDIA remains a Bank of America top pick for June.

### GLDM (SPDR Gold MiniShares)
Gold is under pressure. GLDM was trading around **$85.76 as of the last confirmed data**.

The headwind here is macro: the stronger-than-expected US jobs report from last Friday — which showed May employment growth of approximately **172,000 jobs, roughly double analyst consensus** — pushed Treasury yields higher and strengthened the US dollar. Both of those factors are typically negative for gold, as they reduce the relative appeal of non-yielding assets. Watch for the May Consumer Price Index data due tomorrow, Wednesday June 10, which could either reinforce or dampen the rate-hike narrative and therefore move gold significantly in either direction.

### GOOG (Alphabet / Google)
No major company-specific breaking news for Alphabet today. The broader tech sector recovery on Monday — S&P 500 up 0.8%, Nasdaq 100 up 1.5% — lifted the sector generally. Palantir's deepened integration with Google Cloud's BigQuery and Gemini artificial intelligence products is worth noting as a signal of continued enterprise cloud competition, where Alphabet's cloud division is a key player. No specific price catalyst confirmed for today.

### TSM (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company)
Taiwan Semiconductor is in an excellent position right now. The stock jumped from **$415.17 to $428.89** — a meaningful single-day move — and the fundamental story is compelling.

First quarter 2026 earnings per share came in at **$3.49, beating estimates by 8.4%**, with quarterly revenue up **35.1% year on year**. The company controls roughly **72% of the global foundry market** and is running its advanced nodes near capacity, printing chips for NVIDIA, AMD, Apple, and Broadcom.

The latest strategic news: **Taiwan Semiconductor plans to raise pricing on its 3-nanometer process node by up to 15% in the second half of 2026**, driven by accelerating adoption from NVIDIA, AMD, Google, and Amazon Web Services. When a supplier with this kind of market dominance raises prices, it typically reflects demand so strong that customers have no alternative. Watch for further commentary on this pricing move.

The board also approved approximately **$31.28 billion in capital investment for new fabrication facilities**, including up to **$20 billion for its Arizona operations** — a clear signal of geographic diversification away from Taiwan.

### PLTR (Palantir Technologies)
Palantir continues to be one of the stronger performers in your portfolio right now. The stock is priced at **$137.11**, with a market capitalisation of approximately $328.7 billion.

First quarter 2026 results were exceptional: **revenue of $1.63 billion**, **85% year-on-year growth**, and the company **raised full-year 2026 guidance to $7.65–$7.66 billion** — representing approximately 71% year-on-year growth. US commercial revenue in particular surged with **triple-digit percentage growth**.

Recent partnership announcements include a deepened **Google Cloud integration with BigQuery and Gemini connectivity**, an artificial intelligence-powered private equity platform with law firm **Kirkland & Ellis**, and enterprise deals with GNP Seguros and McCarthy Building Companies.

The one note of caution flagged by analysts: Palantir currently trades at approximately **42 times forward sales**, which is an exceptionally rich valuation. That is not unusual for a high-growth software platform, but it leaves the stock vulnerable if any earnings momentum slows.

### QCOM (Qualcomm)
No major company-specific catalyst found for Qualcomm today. The semiconductor sector recovery on Monday is a positive tailwind — Qualcomm benefits broadly from the continuing rollout of device-level artificial intelligence across the Android ecosystem and its Snapdragon chip dominance. Monitor for any announcements around partnerships or next-generation chip architecture in the coming weeks.

### AAPL (Apple)
Apple had its big moment yesterday at its **2026 Worldwide Developers Conference** — and this is the main story for the stock this week.

The headline announcement: **Siri AI — a fully rebuilt version of Apple's voice assistant** — was revealed. The new Siri can handle natural conversations, answer complex questions, search your device for photos and documents, and has customisable voice pacing and expressiveness. It's Apple's most significant artificial intelligence response to growing competitive pressure from rivals.

Also announced: **iOS 27**, **macOS Golden Gate**, expanded Apple Intelligence features across iPhone, iPad, and Mac, and new family safety tools.

The CEO story is also significant: **Tim Cook is stepping down in September 2026**, handing leadership to **John Ternus**, who currently oversees Apple hardware development. Cook will move into an executive chairman role. This is a major leadership transition for one of the world's most valuable companies — markets will be watching for any signals about strategic direction under Ternus.

One complication to note: **the new Siri AI features will not be available in the European Union** at launch due to regulatory constraints, which could cap the addressable market for the new platform in the near term.

Given that ServiceNow's stock has been under pressure over the past twelve months, any positive artificial intelligence adoption signal could be a meaningful catalyst.

## Section 4 — Major US Market Events

Here's the broader picture for US markets as we head into today.

**Markets recovered solidly on Monday.** After the S&P 500 dropped more than 2.6% on Friday — ending a nine-week winning streak — US equities rebounded on Monday, with the **S&P 500 up 0.8%, the Nasdaq 100 up 1.5%, and the Dow Jones up 0.4%**. The main catalyst was easing tensions in the Middle East and a recovery in technology and semiconductor stocks.

**The May Consumer Price Index report is the event of the week** — and it drops tomorrow, Wednesday June 10, at 8:30 in the morning US Eastern time. This is the single most important data point for markets this week. Context: April's Consumer Price Index came in at **3.8% annually — the highest reading since May 2023** — with energy prices up 17.9% year on year as the biggest driver. If May's reading comes in similarly hot, expect renewed Federal Reserve rate hike fears, pressure on bonds, a stronger dollar, and headwinds for growth stocks. If it cools, markets could push higher again. Essentially, everything pivots around this number tomorrow.

**Federal Reserve rate hike odds are running at approximately 30%** for a hike by year end, according to futures market data. That is not yet the base case, but it is a meaningful probability that markets are actively pricing in.

**Corning (GLW) surged 9.31% in premarket trading on Monday** after Amazon announced a multibillion-dollar agreement to supply optical fibre for its United States data centres. This is a signal of the enormous infrastructure buildout happening around artificial intelligence — the data centre spend cycle remains very much alive.

**Marvell Technology (MRVL) surged approximately 9%** after being announced as a new addition to the S&P 500, joining the benchmark index on June 22. Marvell had already been in the spotlight after NVIDIA chief executive Jensen Huang publicly predicted it would become the next trillion-dollar company last week.

**Semiconductor sector recovery is broadening.** The VanEck Semiconductor exchange-traded fund gained 5% on Monday, though it has not yet recouped all recent losses. With Taiwan Semiconductor's pricing power signal and NVIDIA's SK Hynix partnership fresh in the news, the artificial intelligence chip narrative remains intact.

**Anthropic and SpaceX IPOs remain on the near-term horizon.** Anthropic — the AI company — has filed confidentially for an initial public offering at a near-$1 trillion valuation. SpaceX, targeting a valuation of approximately $1.77 trillion in what would be the largest initial public offering in history, is in an active roadshow phase. Both will continue to draw significant media and institutional investor attention this week, potentially pulling capital flows from existing technology positions.
