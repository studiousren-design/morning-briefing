Starting with your commute from South Woodford to Newham Collegiate Sixth Form Centre.

As always, check the live TfL bus status page at tfl.gov.uk/bus/status just before you leave for any last-minute diversions.

### Train — Tuesday Reminder

**It's Tuesday, so the default recommendation is to take the train.** However, there is an important caveat this morning.

The **Elizabeth line** experienced a service closure from around 2:30 AM, with the expectation that it would resume later this morning — but it may still be subject to residual delays during the peak commute window. The **Central line** — which serves South Woodford directly — has also been affected by severe delays during morning peak hours today.

Given these disruptions, you have two options:

1. **If service has resumed and is running smoothly by the time you leave:** Take the Central line from South Woodford towards Stratford, then continue to East Ham via the District line or a connecting bus. Check tfl.gov.uk/tube-dlr-overground/status before heading out.
2. **If delays are still showing:** Stick with the bus — the W13 to 101 route looks clear and is a reliable fallback this morning.

There is **no Tube or bus strike today**. The next planned RMT Tube driver strike is scheduled for midday Thursday 21 May through to midday Friday 22 May — well worth flagging for next week's planning.

## Section 4: Broader US Stock Market

### The Headline Event Today

The **April Consumer Price Index** releases at **8:30 AM US Eastern time** — this is the single biggest market-moving event of the day, and arguably the week. Consensus expects:

- Headline CPI: **+0.6% month-over-month / +3.7% year-over-year**
- Core CPI: **+0.3% month-over-month / +2.7% year-over-year**

With the **S&P 500 at an all-time high of 7,412.84** and the **Nasdaq Composite at 26,274.13** — both having just set fresh intraday records — the market is trading with very little margin for an upside inflation surprise. A hotter-than-expected print would likely reset Federal Reserve interest rate cut expectations and create meaningful near-term pressure across your technology, semiconductor, and crypto holdings. A soft or in-line print would validate the current bullish posture.

### Broader Context

The S&P 500 has now broken above 7,300 for the first time in history and is in the middle of its longest six-week winning streak since 2024. Earnings growth at the largest technology companies continues to outpace the broader index by more than 40%. However, markets are sensitive to any data that might push rate cut timelines further out.

**US-China trade dynamics** remain a live issue in the background. There has been active market positioning around whether a formal tariff agreement between the US and China can be reached by the end of May 2026 — something odds markets are actively tracking. This has particular relevance for your semiconductor and technology holdings, as Alphabet, Apple, NVIDIA, Qualcomm, and Taiwan Semiconductor all have significant supply chain or revenue exposure to China.
