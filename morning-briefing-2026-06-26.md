### SECTION THREE: YOUR PORTFOLIO — STOCKS TO WATCH

Now, let's run through the major events affecting your portfolio stocks.

**NVIDIA — ticker NVDA**
NVIDIA reported its quarterly earnings on Wednesday, 24 June, and the numbers were strong: quarterly revenue of 44.1 billion dollars, with analysts maintaining a Strong Buy consensus and an average 12-month price target of around 299 dollars. The stock is currently trading around 200 dollars. Despite the solid earnings, the stock fell roughly 1.6% yesterday as part of a broader sell-off in megacap technology names. Analysts remain bullish, with the next earnings date set for 26 August.

**SPDR Gold MiniShares — ticker GLDM**
Gold-tracking exchange-traded funds remain steady. GLDM was last seen trading around 83.44 dollars as of 20 June. There is no major news specific to gold or this fund today, but ongoing macroeconomic uncertainty continues to support gold as a safe-haven asset.

**Alphabet — ticker GOOG**
Alphabet was caught in yesterday's megacap technology sell-off that pushed the Nasdaq Composite down 0.46% for a fourth consecutive losing session. No company-specific news from Alphabet today, but the broader pressure on large-cap technology stocks is worth noting.

**Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company — ticker TSM**
Taiwan Semiconductor is one of the brighter spots in the semiconductor sector. The company reported May revenue of 416.98 billion New Taiwan dollars — up 30.1% year over year and up 1.5% from April. UBS named Taiwan Semiconductor as a top pick, citing strong foundry utilisation rates and an improving memory market. Bank of America raised its price target to 590 dollars. The stock was trading around 435 dollars as of 25 June.

**Palantir Technologies — ticker PLTR**
Palantir has had a rough 2026, down nearly 40% from its highs and flirting with oversold territory near a 52-week low of around 112 dollars. The decline comes despite very strong fundamentals — Q1 2026 revenue grew 85% year over year to 1.63 billion dollars and full-year guidance was raised. The pressure comes from European contract risks: France is phasing out Palantir tools for a domestic alternative, and the UK Parliament is urging the termination of the National Health Service Federated Data Platform deal. Watch this one closely.

**Apple — ticker AAPL**
This is the headline stock story this week. Apple announced price hikes across its Mac and iPad product lines, citing a severe global shortage of memory chips driven by the artificial intelligence data centre boom. The stock fell 6.1% on Thursday to 275.42 dollars. Some models have seen price increases of hundreds of dollars — for example, the MacBook Neo went from 599 to 699 dollars, and the Mac Studio saw a 1,300 dollar price increase. Markets are digesting this as a near-term demand risk heading into the weekend.

**Intuitive Surgical — ticker ISRG**
Intuitive Surgical shares have declined about 28% this year despite strong underlying business performance, with 20.2% revenue growth and solid free cash flow. The stock closed at 401.83 dollars on 24 June. Analysts remain constructive — Piper Sandler kept an Overweight rating, though it trimmed its price target from 620 to 580 dollars. The analyst view is that the selloff is overdone relative to fundamentals.

**Broadridge Financial Solutions — ticker BR**
No specific news was available for Broadridge Financial Solutions today. The stock continues to trade without major company-specific catalysts this week.

**QuantumScape — ticker QS**
QuantumScape has quietly announced a partnership with Honda on solid-state battery technology, potentially expanding use cases beyond electric vehicles. This is a long-term development story — no significant near-term price catalyst today, but it reinforces the company's relevance in next-generation battery development.

**ServiceNow — ticker NOW**
ServiceNow shares are down roughly 40% over the past year, though the company's fundamentals remain strong: fiscal year 2025 revenue of 13.28 billion dollars, a 98% renewal rate, and a board-authorised five billion dollar share buyback with a two billion dollar accelerated repurchase underway. The valuation reset may be creating a long-term entry opportunity, but sentiment remains cautious.

**Qualcomm — ticker QCOM**
Qualcomm surged 6.17% earlier this week on 21 June, driven by optimism ahead of an upcoming Investor Day expected to showcase Qualcomm's strategic pivot away from the mature smartphone chip market toward artificial intelligence infrastructure, edge computing, and next-generation 6G technology. Watch for any Investor Day updates as a potential catalyst.

**Marvell Technology — ticker MRVL**
Marvell is a standout performer. The company announced it has shipped over five million coherent photonic integrated circuits to customers worldwide, in partnership with Tower Semiconductor, addressing data centre component shortages. Marvell joined the S&P 500 index on 22 June and the stock has more than tripled in value this year. This is one of the portfolio's strongest positions right now.

### SECTION FOUR: BROADER US MARKET OVERVIEW

Now let's zoom out to the wider US stock market.

Thursday's session ended mixed after a volatile day. The **Nasdaq Composite fell 0.46%**, extending a four-day losing streak for the index. The **Dow Jones Industrial Average** held up better, but megacap technology stocks broadly weakened.

The dominant market theme right now is a **severe global memory and storage chip shortage**, triggered by the explosion in artificial intelligence data centre construction. Apple and Microsoft both announced consumer hardware price hikes this week — Apple on Macs and iPads, Microsoft on Xbox consoles — blaming the shortage. This is rippling through earnings expectations for hardware and device companies.

The **bright spot** was Micron Technology, which surged 15.7% after reporting strong earnings and an upbeat revenue outlook, lifting broader semiconductor equipment and memory-adjacent names.

**Key economic data today:** The final **University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment** reading for June is due out today. This is the headline economic release for Friday and could move markets — a stronger or weaker-than-expected reading on consumer confidence will be closely watched as investors assess whether the AI and chip supply pressures are filtering through to household sentiment.

The artificial intelligence infrastructure build-out remains the central narrative driving both optimism in semiconductor names — like Marvell and Taiwan Semiconductor — and near-term pressure on device makers like Apple. Expect continued volatility in technology stocks as the market digests the implications of this memory chip shortage.
