## 4. Broader US Market — What to Watch Today

*And finally, the bigger picture for the US market on Thursday.*

### The Big Event: PCE Inflation Data — Released at 8:30 AM US Eastern Time

This is the **most important economic release of the week**. The **Personal Consumption Expenditures Price Index** for May — the Federal Reserve's preferred inflation gauge — dropped this morning. **Wells Fargo economists forecast PCE up 0.5% month on month**, which would push the annual rate to **4.1%** — up from 3.8% in April. Core PCE (excluding food and energy) was expected around **3.3% year on year**.

Why does this matter? Because the Fed under **new Chair Kevin Warsh** has shifted to a more hawkish stance. **Bank of America and Deutsche Bank** both expect the Federal Reserve to **raise interest rates three more times in 2026** — 25 basis points each in September, October, and December. A hotter-than-expected PCE print this morning would reinforce that trajectory and likely pressure equities, particularly rate-sensitive growth stocks. A softer print could offer some relief.
