## Section 3: Your Stocks — Key Events and Movers

### Nvidia (NVDA)
**Major development this morning.** After initially being excluded from President Trump's business delegation to China, Nvidia Chief Executive Officer Jensen Huang has joined the trip as a last-minute addition — Trump reportedly called Huang personally, and Huang flew to Alaska to board Air Force One. This is highly significant: China once accounted for more than a fifth of Nvidia's data centre revenue, and the company has been operating under tightening US export restrictions on its most advanced chips for the past four years. Any signal of improved US-China trade relations on semiconductors could be a meaningful positive catalyst for Nvidia shares. Watch this story closely throughout the day.

### Qualcomm (QCOM)
Qualcomm had a brutal Tuesday — the stock dropped between 11% and 14%, its worst single-day fall since 2020. The selloff was driven by a combination of hotter-than-expected inflation data, geopolitical risk-off sentiment around Iran, and company-specific concerns. Analysts flagged that Qualcomm is losing share in Apple's supply chain, the Android smartphone market remains weak, and competition from Nvidia is growing in the Windows processor segment. The stock had also surged more than 60% in just a few weeks prior, leaving it overextended. Expect continued volatility; watch for any stabilisation or further selling pressure today.

### Taiwan Semiconductor (TSM)
Taiwan Semiconductor reported strong first-quarter 2026 results, with revenue up 35% year over year, powered by artificial intelligence chip demand. The company is planning capital expenditure of between $52 billion and $56 billion for 2026, with fab projects advancing in Taiwan, the United States, and Japan. The stock is up approximately 30% year to date. The ongoing artificial intelligence infrastructure buildout by hyperscalers continues to support a strong demand outlook.

### Alphabet / Google (GOOG)
Alphabet, along with Meta, Amazon, and Microsoft, announced aggregate capital expenditure plans of $725 billion for 2026 — a 77% year-over-year increase — almost entirely directed at artificial intelligence infrastructure. This spending underpins the semiconductor and networking sector demand story and is a structural positive for Alphabet's competitive position in cloud artificial intelligence.

### Meta Platforms (META)
Same headline as Google. Meta is part of the big-four cloud providers committing extraordinary capital to artificial intelligence infrastructure this year. This reinforces the long-term structural demand for compute, networking, and storage — all positive for Meta's competitive positioning.

### Bitcoin (BTC-USD) and iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT)

The broader cryptocurrency market sentiment is cautiously optimistic, with exchange-traded fund inflows recovering and on-chain metrics improving. ## Section 4: Broader US Market — What to Watch Today

**Yesterday's close (Tuesday, 12 May 2026):**
- S&P 500: Closed at 7,400.96, down 0.16%
- Nasdaq Composite: Closed at 26,088.20, down 0.71%
- Chip stocks led declines; energy stocks outperformed on higher oil prices

**What drove it:**
April Consumer Price Index came in hotter than expected at 3.8% year over year (consensus was 3.7%), while core Consumer Price Index — which strips out food and energy — printed at 2.8% versus the 2.7% estimate. This reinforced concerns that the US Federal Reserve may keep interest rates higher for longer, weighing on growth and technology stocks.

**Today's key events:**

1. **Cisco Systems (CSCO) earnings after market close** — This is the headline event today. Cisco reports its third-quarter fiscal 2026 results after the bell. Management has guided for revenue of $15.4 to $15.6 billion and adjusted earnings per share of $1.02 to $1.04, both above initial Street consensus. Cisco has also raised its full-year artificial intelligence infrastructure order outlook to over $5 billion, with second-quarter alone booking $2.1 billion in artificial intelligence orders. A strong print and guidance could lift the broader technology sector. One analyst has flagged a potential 25% post-earnings move.

2. **April Producer Price Index data** — Released today, this will provide another inflation read. Following yesterday's hotter Consumer Price Index, a similarly elevated Producer Price Index could further dampen risk appetite and signal persistent pipeline price pressures for businesses.

3. **Trump-Xi China meetings** — The US business delegation in China, now including Nvidia's Jensen Huang, will be closely watched for any developments on trade policy, particularly regarding semiconductor export restrictions. Any positive signals on chip sales to China would be a major positive catalyst for the semiconductor sector.

4. **Geopolitical backdrop** — Ongoing tensions involving Iran continue to push oil prices higher, benefiting energy stocks while weighing on the broader market's risk appetite.
