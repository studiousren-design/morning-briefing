## SECTION 1: GETTING TO WORK — TRAFFIC & TRANSPORT

### Tube Strike Alert — This is the Big One

First things first: **RMT Tube drivers are on strike today**. The Central line has no service between White City and Liverpool Street. However — and this matters for your commute — services *are* running between Liverpool Street and Woodford via the Hainault loop (every 10 minutes), and between Leytonstone and Epping (every 30 minutes).

South Woodford sits east of Liverpool Street on the Central line, so there is some service in your area, but it will be **significantly reduced and disrupted**. Trains may start late, finish early, and run infrequently before 6:30 in the morning. Transport for London's advice is to travel after 6:30 and complete Underground journeys before 21:00.

The Elizabeth line, Docklands Light Railway, Overground, and buses are expected to run as normal — but expect them to be much busier than usual as Londoners seek alternatives to the Tube.

### Your Bus Route: W13 → 101

No specific disruption has been reported for the W13 between South Woodford and Wanstead. However, **route 101 is currently on diversion**. High Street North in East Ham is closed between Barking Road and Ron Leighton Way, so the 101 is diverting via Barking Road and Ron Leighton Way. This diversion is in place until at least late September, so build in a few extra minutes for the last leg of your journey. The stop locations may differ slightly from usual — keep an eye on the Transport for London real-time bus tracker.

### Recommendation

Given the tube strike and the 101 diversion, your bus route (W13 to Wanstead, then 101 to East Ham) is probably your most reliable option today. Buses should run normally — just expect them to be busier. If you do take the train, you would need to change onto the Docklands Light Railway or Elizabeth line at some point to reach Newham, and both those services are unaffected by the strike.

### NVIDIA (NVDA) — The Star of the Week

NVIDIA remains the story of the moment. Following its announcement at Computex 2026 earlier this week, CEO Jensen Huang unveiled the **RTX Spark superchip** — a single package combining a Blackwell graphics processor and a custom 20-core ARM-based central processing unit co-developed with MediaTek, all built on Taiwan Semiconductor's 3-nanometre process. The chip offers up to 128 gigabytes of unified memory and delivers one petaflop of artificial intelligence performance. More than 30 laptop models and 10 desktop configurations are expected to ship in autumn 2026.

The stock surged roughly 5 to 6 percent on the announcement and is now trading around $222, making NVIDIA the first company ever to reach a $5 trillion market capitalisation. Wall Street remains bullish, with an average analyst price target of $309.94, implying around 39% upside from current levels. On June 3rd, NVDA traded between $220.80 and $232.28.

**Watch for**: Any follow-on Computex news or partner announcements from ASUS or HP, who both unveiled RTX Spark-based machines.

The long-term artificial intelligence thesis remains intact, but near-term earnings pressure is on investors' minds.

### Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing (TSM) — Pricing Power on Display

Taiwan Semiconductor is trading at $446.69 and is on a Strong Buy consensus from 17 analysts. Separately, Taiwan Semiconductor and NVIDIA confirmed a deepening partnership, with the foundry implementing NVIDIA's artificial intelligence technologies in its own semiconductor design and manufacturing operations. The RTX Spark chip is built on Taiwan Semiconductor's 3nm node, which underscores just how central this company is to the AI hardware story.

## SECTION 4: MAJOR US MARKET EVENTS

### The Broader Picture — A Rough Night for Tech

The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell **532 points, or 1.04%, to close at 50,776 points on Wednesday**. The losses were led by IBM (down 7.06%), Honeywell International (down 5.09%), and Salesforce (down 4.92%).

In after-hours trading, US stock futures moved further lower. The main driver was **Broadcom**, which is not in your portfolio but whose earnings ripple across the semiconductor world. Broadcom reported fiscal second-quarter revenue below expectations, and its stock sank nearly 14%. CrowdStrike also fell more than 11% after issuing soft second-quarter sales guidance.

The contagion spread: Intel fell 2%, Advanced Micro Devices fell 2.9%, and Arm Holdings fell 4.3%. The message from markets is clear — while artificial intelligence enthusiasm remains strong, investors are scrutinising execution and profitability more closely.

### US-Iran Tensions — A New Geopolitical Wildcard

Beyond earnings, investors are grappling with a significant geopolitical development: **fresh exchanges of strikes between the United States and Iran**. This has undermined hopes for a peace agreement and is stoking concerns about inflation and higher interest rates. A flare-up in the Middle East typically sends oil prices higher, which feeds into inflation expectations, which in turn puts pressure on rate-sensitive assets. Watch oil prices and gold carefully in the coming days. This is a situation that could evolve quickly.
