## Section 5: Broader US Market — What's Moving Today

There is a **lot** happening in the market today. Here are the top macro themes to watch:

### Consumer Price Index Report — LIVE TODAY
This is the single biggest market event of the day. The **Consumer Price Index for May 2026** is being released at **8:30 AM Eastern Time** — that's **1:30 PM London time**. Markets are on edge. April's Consumer Price Index came in at 3.8%, above forecasts, with energy costs up 17.9% year on year — driven by the Iran conflict. Core inflation edged up to 2.8%. Professional forecasters are now projecting **headline inflation could hit 6% in the second quarter**. If today's May print surprises to the upside, expect a sharp reaction across equities, bonds, and gold.

### US Military Strikes Against Iran — Geopolitical Risk Elevated
This is the elephant in the room. The US military launched what were described as **self-defence strikes against Iran**, following Iranian retaliation. On Monday the 9th, the Nasdaq and S&P 500 fell sharply, with tech and energy stocks bearing the brunt. Oil prices are elevated, feeding directly into the inflation story above. Watch energy sector stocks and defence names closely.

### Semiconductor Sector Rebound
After Friday's heavy tech selloff, **chip stocks are rebounding** this week. Marvell Technology and Micron led the recovery Monday, with NVIDIA also recovering ground. NVIDIA confirmed a **partnership with South Korea's SK Hynix** for next-generation memory development aligned with NVIDIA's artificial intelligence infrastructure roadmap. Semiconductor supply chain news remains highly market-moving right now.

A hot print could even open the door to rate hike discussions, which would be a significant headwind for growth stocks.
