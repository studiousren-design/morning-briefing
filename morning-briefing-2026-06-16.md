## Section 5 — Major US Market Events This Week

**The dominant story: a US–Iran peace deal lifted the whole market yesterday.**

The Trump administration announced that a memorandum of understanding with Iran was complete, with a formal signing expected in Switzerland and the Strait of Hormuz set to reopen without a toll system. Markets reacted strongly on the 15th of June — the Dow Jones Industrial Average rose about 1% to roughly 51,928, the S&P 500 gained around 1.9% to about 7,573, and the Nasdaq Composite jumped 3% to roughly 26,667. Technology and semiconductor stocks led gains as risk appetite returned across the board.

**The Federal Reserve's two-day meeting began today, with the decision due tomorrow.**

Today, the 16th of June, marks the start of new Federal Reserve Chair Kevin Warsh's first policy meeting, running through the 17th. Markets are pricing in roughly a 97% chance the Fed holds rates steady, though longer-term odds — around 70% by some estimates — point to a possible rate hike by December, given that inflation has climbed to 4.2% year-on-year in May, the highest reading since 2023. Warsh's tone in his first post-meeting press conference will be closely watched for signs of a hawkish or dovish stance.

**A broadly positive week so far, with the Fed decision as the next catalyst.**

The combination of the Iran peace deal and renewed risk appetite has set a positive tone for markets this week. The main thing to watch from here is tomorrow's Federal Reserve statement and press conference — any hawkish surprise on inflation could quickly reverse some of this week's gains, particularly in higher-multiple technology and AI-related names.
