## 1. Getting to Work: South Woodford to Newham Collegiate Sixth Form Centre

**Big news this morning — there is a 48-hour London Underground strike in effect.** The industrial action, called by the Rail, Maritime and Transport union, began yesterday evening and runs through to the morning of Friday, 8 May. The **Central line** — which serves your local South Woodford station — is among the most severely affected, with very limited or no service east of Liverpool Street. That means your tube option is effectively off the table today.

**The good news:** your usual bus route — the **W13 from South Woodford to Wanstead, then the 101 from Wanstead to East Ham** — is operating normally. Transport for London has confirmed buses are running to their regular schedules during the strike. However, brace yourself for significantly higher passenger numbers. Displaced tube commuters across East London will be flooding onto buses, so expect crowding, possible standing-room-only conditions, and some knock-on delays at busy stops.

**Practical advice for this morning:**
- Leave earlier than usual — allow an extra 20 to 30 minutes on top of your normal journey time.
- Try to board the W13 at South Woodford rather than waiting for a later one at Woodford Green, as buses may be full by the time they reach further stops.
- The Elizabeth line and London Overground are running normally if your journey allows a connection to either of those.
- Transport for London recommends completing all journeys by 8pm today to avoid the worst of evening disruption as the strike winds down.
- No specific diversions or road closures were found for the W13 or 101 routes this morning beyond the general strike-related crowding.

*Today is Thursday, so no special train reminder — that's only for Tuesdays. But given the Central line is on strike, the bus is your friend today regardless.*

## 3. Your Portfolio: Stock and Asset Watch

Here is what is moving or worth watching for the tickers in your portfolio today:

### NVIDIA (NVDA)
Nvidia remains the elephant in the room for the market. The company is not reporting earnings until **20 May**, but analyst expectations are sky-high — Wall Street is forecasting approximately **78.8 billion dollars in revenue** for Q1, up nearly 79 percent year over year, and earnings per share of $1.77, more than doubling from last year. The catalyst fuelling that outlook is continued massive capital expenditure announcements from hyperscalers: Meta has guided capex of up to **145 billion dollars** for 2026, and Microsoft has announced **190 billion dollars** in planned infrastructure spending. These are direct revenue tailwinds for Nvidia's data centre division. The stock trades at a price-to-earnings ratio of around 40.5, which is actually a notable discount to its own 10-year average of 61.7 — an interesting dynamic heading into that earnings print.

### SPDR Gold MiniShares (GLDM)
Gold is steady this week. GLDM was last seen trading at around **$90.78 per share**, with the broader SPDR Gold Trust (GLD) at approximately $431. Gold has held its value as a safe haven, particularly given ongoing geopolitical developments. No sharp moves in either direction this morning, but watch for any surprises from the Iran-US peace talks (more on that below under market events), as a breakthrough could send gold lower alongside oil.

### Alphabet / Google (GOOG)
Alphabet is on an absolute tear. The company reported **Q1 2026 revenue growth of 22 percent** and net income growth of **84 percent**, and its market capitalisation has climbed to approximately **4.76 trillion dollars**. Alphabet reached an all-time high share price of $383.39 just last week on 1 May, and the company is now closing the gap on Nvidia as the world's most valuable company. Strong advertising revenue and its growing cloud and artificial intelligence business are driving momentum. This one continues to look solid.

### Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing (TSM)
TSMC had a standout session on Wednesday, surging **6 percent to hit a fresh 52-week high of $418.03**. The catalyst was Advanced Micro Devices' blowout Q1 2026 earnings — revenue of $10.3 billion, up 38 percent year on year — which underscored roaring demand for the semiconductors TSMC manufactures. TSMC's own guidance is bullish: its 2-nanometer chip capacity is expected to grow at a compounded annual rate of **70 percent** from this year through 2028. Capital spending is on track to hit the upper end of their $52 to $56 billion target for the year. Chip demand from artificial intelligence applications is described by the CEO as "extremely robust."

### Bitcoin (BTC-USD)
Bitcoin is trading at around **$81,437**, up approximately half a percent over the past 24 hours and nearly 6 percent over the past week. The sentiment drivers are very positive right now. The US Congress is advancing the CLARITY Act — a key piece of crypto market structure legislation — toward committee debate in May. At the Bitcoin 2026 Conference, a White House adviser also teased an imminent announcement around a **Strategic Bitcoin Reserve**, which would formalise a legal framework for the US government to hold seized Bitcoin as a long-term strategic asset. That kind of institutional and regulatory momentum is supportive for the asset class.

### Meta Platforms (META)
Meta reported a blockbuster first quarter. **Revenue of $56.3 billion**, up 33 percent year on year. **Earnings per share of $10.44**, up 61 percent. The numbers are impressive by any measure. However, investors are keeping a close eye on the spending side: Meta's capital expenditure hit a record $72.2 billion in 2025 and management expects it to explode to as much as **$145 billion in 2026**. That is an extraordinary level of infrastructure investment, and while it is being made in service of long-term artificial intelligence leadership, it introduces meaningful earnings uncertainty if the revenue growth does not keep pace.

Palantir also reported recently, posting **Q1 2026 revenue growth of 85 percent** year on year — a genuinely exceptional result. The stock, however, sold off sharply in response, falling nearly **7 percent** in the session after the report. This is a familiar pattern for Palantir: the market prices in near-perfection, and even strong results get punished if they are not accompanied by an upward revision to forward guidance. The stock is currently trading in the range of **$144 to $149**. High growth, high valuation, high volatility — proceed with your eyes open.

## 4. Broader US Market: What Is Moving the Needle

Wednesday's session was a strong one for US equities. Here is the big picture:

**Markets hit new records.** The S&P 500 advanced 1.46 percent to close at **7,365** — a new all-time high. The Nasdaq Composite gained 2.02 percent to **25,838** — also a new record. The Dow Jones Industrial Average added over 600 points, or 1.24 percent, closing at **49,910**.

**The Iran-US peace talks are the dominant macro story.** Reports emerged Wednesday that the United States and Iran are nearing a peace agreement to end their conflict. This sent **oil prices plunging** — West Texas Intermediate crude fell nearly **9 percent to around $93 per barrel**, and international Brent crude dropped 7.7 percent to near $101. For equity markets, the prospect of lower energy costs and reduced geopolitical risk is a significant positive. However, be aware that these negotiations can break down quickly. Watch for further developments here as they could move markets sharply in either direction.

**The Federal Reserve is in a holding pattern.** The Fed held its benchmark interest rate steady at **3.5 to 3.75 percent** at its most recent meeting — the third consecutive hold. The tone of the decision was described as more cautious than previous statements, suggesting the central bank remains wary of inflation re-acceleration even as it resists cutting rates. No imminent rate cut is expected.

**Earnings season continues to impress.** With approximately one-third of S&P 500 companies having reported Q1 results, the beat rate stands at **84 percent**, with the average upside surprise of **12.3 percent** over estimates. This is materially above historical averages and provides a strong fundamental underpinning for current market valuations.

**Key risk to watch:** The Iran-US peace narrative is the biggest swing factor this week. Any breakdown in talks could reverse Wednesday's oil price drop and re-inject geopolitical risk premium into equity markets.
