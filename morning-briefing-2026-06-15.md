## Section 5 — Major US Market Events This Week

**The big story: SpaceX goes public in the largest initial public offering in history.**

SpaceX raised **$75 billion** at $135 per share on June 11th, listing on the Nasdaq under the ticker SPCX. On its first day of trading, June 12th, shares jumped 19% — valuing the company at around $2.2 trillion and placing it alongside Nvidia, Apple, Alphabet, Microsoft, and Amazon in the exclusive $2 trillion market cap club. Analysts note this is the first of potentially three giant artificial intelligence-adjacent initial public offerings — OpenAI and Anthropic have also filed paperwork with the US Securities and Exchange Commission. If SpaceX's debut is any signal, investor appetite for these listings is strong.

---

**The Federal Reserve meets this week — but expect no surprises.**

The Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) begins its two-day meeting **today, June 16–17**, with a rate decision expected Wednesday. Market-implied probability of no change to interest rates stands at approximately 99.5%. The federal funds rate remains at 3.5–3.75%. Why no cut? Inflation has climbed back above 4% year-on-year for the first time in three years. New Federal Reserve Chair Kevin Warsh, who took over in May, has signalled a data-dependent, cautious stance — and the data are not pointing toward easing. First theoretical probability of a rate hike has been pushed to March 2027.

**Economic data out today (Monday, June 15):**
- Empire State Manufacturing Index

- Capacity Utilisation
- National Association of Home Builders Housing Market Index

These releases this morning could set the tone for the week before the Fed announcement on Wednesday.
