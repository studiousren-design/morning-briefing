## Section 4: Broader US Market Events

*Finally, the big picture for the US market today.*

**Yesterday's session was rough.** The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell **953 points**, closing at 49,918.78. The S&P 500 closed at 7,266.99. The volatility index — the so-called fear gauge — jumped 10%.

**Why the sell-off?**
Two main drivers:

1. **Inflation data:** The May Consumer Price Index came in showing US inflation at a **three-year high of 4.2%**. This significantly dampens hopes for near-term Federal Reserve interest rate cuts and rattled bond and equity markets alike.

2. **Geopolitical escalation:** Reports that the **United States is conducting strikes on positions in Iran** have added a layer of geopolitical risk premium to markets. Industrials, technology, and cyclical sectors bore the brunt of the selling.

**What to watch today, June 11:**
- **European Central Bank interest rate decision** — a key policy signal that could move global bond markets and spill into US equities.
- **US May Producer Price Index release** — the follow-on to yesterday's hot Consumer Price Index. If this also prints hot, expect continued selling pressure.
- **Earnings: Adobe (ADBE) and Lennar (LEN)** report after market close — technology software and homebuilding respectively, two sectors sensitive to interest rate expectations.

In short: markets are navigating a difficult combination of stubborn inflation, geopolitical risk, and high valuations. Defensive positioning and gold (despite today's dip) remain relevant considerations.
