## Section 5 — Broader US Stock Market

**The key macro backdrop this week is complex but broadly cautious.**

**Moody's credit downgrade** — Late last week, Moody's became the third and final major ratings agency to downgrade US sovereign debt, cutting it one notch from AAA to Aa1. The stated reasons were a decade-plus of rising government debt and interest payment ratios that now significantly exceed those of similarly rated countries. Monday's market reaction was muted — the S&P 500 ended nearly flat (+0.09%), the Dow Jones added 130 points (+0.32%), and the Nasdaq inched up 0.02%. Treasury yields ticked up slightly to around 4.46% on the 10-year. Wall Street's near-term read: a non-event. But the longer-term structural signal on US debt sustainability is less comfortable.

**Inflation running hotter than expected** — April's Consumer Price Index came in at 3.8% year-over-year, the hottest reading since 2023, driven partly by energy prices. The probability of a Federal Reserve interest rate hike at some point in 2026 has now jumped to 45%, up from just 1% a month ago. This is a meaningful shift in rate expectations and is the single biggest macro headwind for equities right now — particularly for growth and technology stocks.

**Federal Reserve leadership transition** — Kevin Warsh is preparing to take the chair of the Federal Reserve following the end of Jerome Powell's term on 15 May. Markets will be watching closely for any signal of a policy direction shift under the new leadership.

**NVIDIA earnings tomorrow dominate the calendar** — With a potential implied move of 8–10%, Wednesday evening's NVIDIA print is the single most market-moving event of the week. A beat could lift the broader technology sector and indices meaningfully; a miss or cautious guidance could pull the whole market lower.

**Broader earnings backdrop remains strong** — First-quarter 2026 earnings overall have grown 27% year-over-year, the strongest pace since 2004 (excluding pandemic recovery periods). This fundamental support is providing a floor beneath equities even as macro concerns mount.
