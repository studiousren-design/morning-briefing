*Now for the big macro picture going into today's session.*

**It's the first trading day of Q3 2026** — and US equities are entering the new quarter from a position of strength. Here's where things stand:

**Q2 2026 performance:**
- **S&P 500**: up over **14%** for the quarter
- **Nasdaq 100**: up approximately **20%**
- **Dow Jones Industrial Average**: up over **12%**, closing at a record high of **52,319** on June 30

**Key data release today:**
The **Institute for Supply Management Manufacturing Purchasing Managers' Index** for June 2026 — released at **10:00 AM Eastern Time** — came in at **54.0**, beating the forecast of 53.3 and up from 52.7 in May. A reading above 50 signals expansion, and 54 represents the strongest factory-sector performance since May 2022. This is a bullish signal for industrial and manufacturing-exposed stocks.

Fed Chair **Kevin Warsh** is speaking at the **European Central Bank Forum in Sintra, Portugal** today. Markets will be listening closely for any signals on the interest rate path. The Fed has noted that economic activity is expanding at a solid pace, but inflation remains above the 2% target — partly due to supply shocks in sectors including energy. Any dovish commentary from Warsh could provide a further lift to equities; hawkish signals could temper enthusiasm.
