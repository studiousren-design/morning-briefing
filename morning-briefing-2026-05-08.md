## 🎙️ SECTION 5 — Broader US Market: What Else Is Moving?

Three big themes for US markets today:

### 1. April Nonfarm Payrolls — The Day's Key Data Release
The US Bureau of Labor Statistics releases the **April 2026 employment report at 8:30 AM Eastern Time** — that is 1:30 PM London time. Estimates vary widely, from 55,000 to 165,000 new jobs (the previous month came in at roughly 175,000–178,000). A weak number would revive recession fears and rate-cut expectations; a strong number could push back rate-cut timelines. This is the single biggest scheduled market catalyst of the day.

### 2. Iran–United States Tensions in the Strait of Hormuz
Markets opened cautiously this morning after a **deterioration in US–Iran relations** over the Strait of Hormuz undid a week of diplomatic progress. The S&P 500 is trading in a narrow 25-point range, with small-cap stocks lagging. Oil markets are reacting, but the impact has so far been contained. This is one to watch through the day — any escalation could spark a risk-off move.

### 3. Federal Reserve Leadership Transition
The **Senate vote on Kevin Warsh to replace Jerome Powell** as Chair of the Federal Reserve is expected around **15 May**. Markets are closely watching the transition for signals on future interest rate policy. Warsh is generally considered to be more hawkish than Powell, which adds uncertainty to the rate outlook.
