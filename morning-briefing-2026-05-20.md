## Section 1: Getting to Work — Travel Update

*Right, let's start with the commute. Two things to flag this morning.*

**Bus Route 101 — Diversion in East Ham:**
There is an active road closure on High Street North between Barking Road and Ron Leighton Way in East Ham Town Centre. As a result, **route 101 is diverting** via Barking Road and Ron Leighton Way. Your usual stop J — Caledon Road — will **not be served**. Instead, board at bus stop BS on Ron Leighton Way. Plan for a slightly longer walk at the East Ham end. Routes 147, 238, 300, 304, 325, 376, and 474 are also affected, so expect heavier bus traffic on the diversion route.

**W13 to Wanstead:** No reported disruptions. Should be a smooth ride from South Woodford to Wanstead this morning.

**Train Option:** Good news on this front — the Rail, Maritime and Transport union strikes that were scheduled for Tuesday the 19th and Wednesday the 20th of May have been **suspended** following last-minute talks with Transport for London. Both the Central line and the Elizabeth line are operating normally today. If you'd rather skip the 101 diversion drama, hopping on the train is a perfectly good option this morning.

## Section 3: Stocks to Watch

*Now let's talk money. Here are the major events affecting your portfolio today.*

### NVDA — NVIDIA Corporation
**This is the big one today.** NVIDIA reports its fiscal first quarter 2027 earnings **after the US market close this evening**, typically around 4:20pm to 4:30pm Eastern time — that's approximately 9:20pm to 9:30pm UK time. Analyst consensus expects earnings per share of **$1.76 to $1.78**, up roughly **120% year-over-year**, on revenue of approximately **$79 billion**, which would represent 79.5% growth from the same quarter last year. Data centre revenue alone is projected at around **$72.85 billion**, nearly double last year's figure. NVIDIA has also recently received regulatory approval to sell chips to select Chinese companies. Given NVIDIA's position as the bellwether of artificial intelligence infrastructure, its market capitalisation could swing by as much as **$355 billion** on the release. This will move the entire technology sector.

### GOOG — Alphabet / Google
Alphabet has announced a **cash dividend of $0.22 per share**, with an ex-dividend date of 8 June 2026. Separately, Google has unveiled **artificial intelligence-powered smart glasses**, putting it in direct competition with Meta and Apple in the wearables space. Google has also entered a **joint venture with Blackstone for cloud computing**. A busy week for the company.

### TSM — Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company
TSMC continues to dominate. **61% of its first quarter 2026 revenue** came from artificial intelligence and high-performance computing — a direct beneficiary of NVIDIA's enormous chip demand. Today's NVIDIA earnings will be closely watched for guidance on future chip orders, which flows directly into TSMC's revenue outlook.

### QCOM — Qualcomm
Qualcomm had a strong earnings beat in its second quarter with revenue of **$10.6 billion**, driven by **record Automotive segment growth of 38%** year-over-year and Internet of Things expansion of 9%. The stock had surged over 50% from its March low to around $200. However, the semiconductor rally cooled sharply, with shares dropping more than **11% on 12 May** and another **6% on 14 May** amid broader risk-off sentiment. Price was around **$195.60** as of yesterday. Some analysts suggest Qualcomm bears may be misreading the rebound potential.

### GLDM — SPDR Gold MiniShares Trust
Gold has been a strong performer, with GLDM posting a **total return of 43.02% over the past year**. The fund's assets under management stand at **$31.93 billion** as of early May 2026. The structural bull case for gold remains intact, underpinned by sustained central bank buying — projected at 500 to 800 tonnes per quarter — and investor reallocation amid uncertainty around Federal Reserve policy under new leadership.

### BTC-USD and IBIT — Bitcoin and iShares Bitcoin Trust
Bitcoin pulled back to approximately **$76,685** as of yesterday, down from a recent high of **$80,120** on 15 May. The catalyst: Iran geopolitical tensions triggered a sharp drop below $77,000, forcing over **$657 million in crypto liquidations** within a 24-hour window. Adding to sector pressure, **Bitcoin Depot** — one of the largest US cryptocurrency ATM operators — filed for **Chapter 11 bankruptcy** on 18 May. On the structural side, spot Bitcoin exchange-traded funds now collectively hold over **$107 billion** in assets, with companies like MicroStrategy holding 818,869 BTC. IBIT, the BlackRock Bitcoin exchange-traded fund, tracks Bitcoin directly and will move with these price swings.

### PLTR — Palantir Technologies
Palantir is navigating a complex landscape: strong revenue growth projections continue, but the company is dealing with **legal challenges and an arbitration ruling** that may affect its competitive position in artificial intelligence services. Autocallable structured products linked to Palantir stock have recently launched, reflecting high institutional interest in volatility plays on this name.

### ISRG — Intuitive Surgical
Despite being down **23.7% year-to-date**, Intuitive Surgical delivered a strong first quarter: **17% procedure growth** and **23% revenue growth**, with robust adoption of its da Vinci 5 system. The stock bounced **3.76% on 18 May**. The company is guiding for **13.5% to 15.5% worldwide da Vinci procedure growth in 2026**. Analysts maintain a consensus moderate buy rating.

### META — Meta Platforms
Meta is competing directly in the artificial intelligence-powered smart glasses space following Google's unveiling this week. No major company-specific announcements today, but NVIDIA's earnings will provide broader signals on artificial intelligence infrastructure spending that matters greatly to Meta's capital expenditure story.

### AAPL — Apple
No major Apple-specific catalyst today. NVIDIA's earnings will be watched for signals on the broader technology sector spending environment. Apple remains in comparative analyses with peers.

### IXN — iShares Global Technology Exchange-Traded Fund
This broad technology exchange-traded fund will be significantly influenced by NVIDIA's after-hours results tonight. Given NVIDIA is a major holding, expect pre-market moves tomorrow to reflect however NVIDIA performs after the bell.

### COIN — Coinbase Global
Coinbase will be sensitive to Bitcoin's continued volatility. The $657 million in crypto liquidations and the Bitcoin Depot bankruptcy are sector headwinds. No specific Coinbase news found for today.

## Section 4: Broader US Market Events

*Stepping back to the macro picture now.*

**NVIDIA Earnings After Close:** As already covered, this is the single biggest market event of the day. It will set the tone for technology and, given technology's weight in major indices, the broader market tomorrow.

**Busy Economic Data Day:** May 20th has approximately **106 economic data releases** scheduled in the United States — an unusually heavy calendar. Traders will be parsing these for clues on inflation, consumer demand, and the Federal Reserve's next steps.

**New Federal Reserve Chair — Kevin Warsh:** The Senate has confirmed **Kevin Warsh** as the next Chair of the Federal Reserve, following Jerome Powell's departure. The Fed held interest rates steady at **3.5% to 3.75%** at its April meeting amid an unusually divided committee. Markets are pricing in one to two rate cuts later in 2026 once Warsh is fully established in the role.

**Corporate Earnings Momentum:** First quarter 2026 earnings season has been exceptional — results came in **9.2% ahead of expectations**, the strongest year-over-year growth since 2004 outside of post-recession recoveries. 84% of S&P 500 companies beat earnings per share estimates, and 81% beat revenue estimates. The forward price-to-earnings ratio for the S&P 500 sits at **20.9**, above both its five-year average of 19.9 and its ten-year average of 18.9.

**Equity Index Pressure:** The Russell 2000 (small-caps) has dropped to **2,725 index points**, its lowest level since April 2026. The Dow Jones is showing **losses of approximately 297 points**, with Sherwin-Williams as a notable drag. Broader risk-off sentiment has been pressuring equities this week.

**Consumer Spending Strong:** Bank of America's internal card data shows spending per household rose **4.8% year-over-year in April**, the strongest pace in three years — a positive signal for consumer-facing businesses.
