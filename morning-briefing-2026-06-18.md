## Section 1: Getting to Work — South Woodford to Newham Collegiate Sixth Form Centre

Here's the good news first: the RMT tube strikes that had been pencilled in for today have been **called off**. Drivers were originally scheduled to walk out on the 16th and the 18th of June as part of an ongoing dispute over working hours — specifically around the introduction of a compressed four-day working week. Both dates of action were abandoned, and Transport for London confirms the Central line and Elizabeth line are running with good service this morning.

For your usual route — the **W13 to Wanstead, then the 101 to East Ham** — there are no reported major disruptions on either bus route as of this morning. You should be able to travel as normal on the buses.

If you fancy taking the train instead, the all-clear on the Underground means that's also a smooth option today, with no service disruption on either the Central line or the Elizabeth line.

**Summary: Green light on all routes. Buses and trains are running normally.**

## Section 4: US Stock Market — The Bigger Picture

The dominant story right now is the **Federal Reserve's hawkish turn**. Fed Chair **Kevin Warsh**, in his first press conference as Chair, struck a firm tone on price stability. The Federal Open Market Committee's median projection for the year-end federal funds rate has moved up to **3.8%**, from 3.4% back in March. That suggests at least one rate hike is now the base case for 2026.

Markets sold off sharply on Wednesday in response — the S&P 500 at -1.21%, the Nasdaq at -1.34%, and the Dow at -0.98%. Semiconductor names bore the brunt of it.

The other big market story of the month is the **SpaceX initial public offering**. SpaceX debuted on the Nasdaq on June 12th at a $1.75 trillion valuation — the largest initial public offering in history. The stock surged over 19% on its debut day and was up a further 3% or more in premarket trading on Wednesday. SpaceX's market capitalisation has already surpassed Amazon's, and the market is watching whether capital rotation out of existing technology names into SpaceX continues.

Looking ahead to today's session: **earnings reports from Accenture and Kroger** are expected. And a heads-up — **US markets will be closed tomorrow, Friday the 19th of June, for the Juneteenth national holiday**. That means today is effectively the last trading day of the week.
