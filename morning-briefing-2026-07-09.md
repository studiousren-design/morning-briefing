## 3. Stocks to Watch: Your Portfolio in Focus

*Now let's talk markets. Here's the rundown on every ticker you're watching.*

### NVDA — NVIDIA Corporation
Big news out overnight: **China is preparing to allow selected technology companies to purchase NVIDIA's H200 artificial intelligence chips** — companies including Alibaba, ByteDance, and DeepSeek. The approval covers fewer than 200,000 chips (well below what Chinese firms had wanted), and usage is restricted to training AI models. Shares climbed roughly 1.5% on the news. NVIDIA's forward price-to-earnings ratio has dropped to 22.22x — the lowest since 2019 — despite reporting record revenues of $215.9 billion. This is a meaningful, if capped, re-entry into the Chinese market.

### GLDM — SPDR Gold MiniShares
Gold's been on a wild ride in 2026. After hitting an all-time high of over $5,300 per troy ounce in January, the spot price has corrected sharply, now sitting closer to the $4,100 level. **GLDM was trading at $81.41 as of yesterday**, within a 52-week range of $64.71 to $109.74. With US-Iran tensions escalating and oil prices rising, gold could see renewed safe-haven demand. Watch this one closely.

### GOOG — Alphabet / Google
A setback for Alphabet: **the European Union's top court upheld a record €4.1 billion (roughly $4.7 billion) fine** against Google for anticompetitive practices tied to its Android operating system. The ruling centred on how Google leveraged Android's dominance to entrench its search engine and app ecosystem. This adds to an already heavy list of legal headwinds — Alphabet is also fighting adtech remedies that could force it to divest its AdX exchange, facing EU investigations into how it uses web publishers' content for artificial intelligence training, and dealing with publisher lawsuits from major names including Condé Nast, Vox Media, and The Atlantic. On the positive side, Alphabet reported revenue of $109.9 billion, up 22% year-over-year, and launched a new Gemini Omni Flash video model. Stock is trading around $356–$358.

### TSM — Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company
Strong tailwinds. Analysts — including Citi, who raised their target to NT 3,800 — are backing Taiwan Semiconductor as it lifts its own revenue growth outlook on the back of robust artificial intelligence chip demand. Over 30% revenue growth is expected for 2026. No specific disruptions today.

### PLTR — Palantir Technologies
Palantir has had a strong couple of weeks. **The US Army selected Palantir Foundry as the cloud data layer for its flagship modernisation programme, NGC2**, taking it from prototype to broad deployment. The company also raised its 2026 revenue guidance to between $7.650 billion and $7.662 billion. The stock is up 14.5% since June 26th and sits around $129.30. This remains one of the more momentum-driven names in your portfolio right now.

### AAPL — Apple Inc.
Apple's 52-week high is $317.40 (reached on June 8th). The major news here is a **newly announced chip supply deal with Broadcom, expected to exceed $30 billion**, covering chip manufacturing in the US through 2031, supporting hundreds of American jobs. Apple has also introduced its own custom 5G modem chips — the C1 and C1X — moving away from Qualcomm for that component. No major share-price catalysts specific to today, but the Broadcom deal is a significant long-term manufacturing story.

This is one of the stronger-performing names heading into earnings season.

Qualcomm is navigating a shift in its relationship with Apple. Apple has developed its own in-house 5G modem chips (the C1 and C1X), reducing its reliance on Qualcomm's Snapdragon modems. Apple's new large chip agreement announced this week is with Broadcom, not Qualcomm. ## 4 & 5. Broader US Market: Geopolitics Take Centre Stage

*And now the big picture.*

The major driver for US markets right now is **escalating military conflict between the United States and Iran**. On Tuesday, US forces carried out strikes against Iran following attacks on three commercial vessels in the Strait of Hormuz. President Trump, speaking from Ankara ahead of a NATO summit, declared that the US-Iran ceasefire memorandum of understanding was "over."

Here's what this means for markets:

- **Oil is surging.** The Strait of Hormuz carries roughly 20% of the world's oil supply. Disruption there sends prices sharply higher. Brent crude has been above $100 per barrel at points during this conflict, and US petrol prices have climbed above $4 per gallon.

- **On Wednesday, the Dow Jones Industrial Average fell roughly 1% (over 500 points), the S&P 500 dropped 0.2%, but the Nasdaq Composite trimmed losses to gain 0.2%**, with technology holding up better as investors rotated into software.

- **Inflation risk is real.** Higher energy prices feed directly into Consumer Price Index readings. Morgan Stanley analysis suggests global inflation could rise more than one percentage point under a sustained elevated oil price scenario, which in turn puts pressure on interest rate expectations.

- **The semiconductor sector is under additional pressure** from Samsung's disappointing earnings, which has created headwinds across chip names.

- **In a more positive note**, SpaceX is set to join the Nasdaq 100, and energy firm TeraWulf's shares jumped over 16% after Anthropic signed a 20-year deal to use its Kentucky data centre for artificial intelligence workloads.

**The macro environment is cautious.** Geopolitics, elevated oil, and upcoming earnings season for several of your holdings (ISRG on July 16th, QS and NOW on July 22nd) make this a week to watch positions carefully rather than make big moves.
