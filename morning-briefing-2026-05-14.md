## Section 4: Wider US Market Events

Here are the bigger macro themes that could move markets today:

**1. Trump-Xi Summit (14–15 May)**
The headline event of the week. President Trump is meeting with Chinese President Xi Jinping, with technology, artificial intelligence guardrails, and trade tariffs all on the agenda. Semiconductor and technology names — particularly NVIDIA, Apple, Taiwan Semiconductor, and Qualcomm — are most exposed to any outcome. A positive breakthrough could fuel another leg higher in the Nasdaq. A breakdown in talks could reverse yesterday's gains rapidly.

**2. April Retail Sales Data — Released Today**
April retail sales figures are due out today. Following Wednesday's alarming Producer Price Index reading — where wholesale prices surged **1.4% for the month** (against a consensus estimate of 0.4%) — investors will be scrutinising retail sales for signs of whether consumers are absorbing higher costs or pulling back on spending.

**3. Applied Materials (AMAT) Earnings**
Semiconductor equipment maker Applied Materials reports earnings today. The market is pricing in earnings per share of $2.68 (up roughly 12% year-on-year), with options markets expecting a plus-or-minus 8.7% move. As a key supplier to the chip industry, AMAT results serve as a broader read on capital spending in the semiconductor sector — relevant to NVIDIA, Taiwan Semiconductor, and Qualcomm.

Wednesday's Producer Price Index shock — core Producer Price Index up 1% versus a 0.3% estimate — has reignited inflation concerns. While the S&P 500 and Nasdaq still hit record highs, bond markets and rate-sensitive sectors are under pressure. The Federal Reserve's next move becomes harder to predict if inflation re-accelerates.
