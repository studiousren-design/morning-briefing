**Share price: approximately $196.98 as of yesterday's close.**

## Section 5: Broader United States Stock Market Events

**The single biggest event for markets today is the release of the Federal Reserve's Federal Open Market Committee meeting minutes at 2 p.m. Eastern Time** — that's 7 p.m. London time this evening.

These are the minutes from the 16–17 June meeting, the first chaired by new Federal Reserve Chair Kevin Warsh. The June meeting was more hawkish than many investors expected. Chair Warsh removed traditional forward guidance from Fed communications and moved to what he described as "pure data dependence." The dot plot — which shows where policymakers expect rates to go — shifted toward a possible rate hike rather than a cut.

Current context:
- The Federal funds rate sits at **3.50% to 3.75%**
- United States inflation (Consumer Price Index) rose **4.2% year on year** through May 2026, the highest since April 2023
- Markets currently price in a **56% probability of a rate hike by September**
- There are **64 economic data releases scheduled for today** across global markets

What this means for your portfolio: if the minutes read hawkish — hinting at further rate increases — expect pressure on growth and technology stocks (NVDA, PLTR, NOW, MRVL, GOOG) and a headwind for gold (GLDM). A dovish reading, or any hint that the Fed is finished hiking, would likely produce a relief rally.

Additionally, **earnings season is building**. Several of your holdings report in the next three weeks:
- **16 July**: TSMC (TSM) and Intuitive Surgical (ISRG)
- **22 July**: ServiceNow (NOW)
- **29 July**: Qualcomm (QCOM)
- **30 July**: Apple (AAPL)

Expect pre-earnings positioning to influence prices this week and next.
