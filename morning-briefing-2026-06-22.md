## Section 4 — Your Portfolio: Stock News Roundup

*Let's run through the major events affecting your stocks today.*

**NVIDIA (NVDA) — ~$210.33**
Big week ahead for NVIDIA. The company just unveiled the **RTX Spark superchip** at Computex in Taipei — a combined Blackwell graphics processor and N1X/N1 processor aimed at bringing artificial intelligence to personal computers. Jensen Huang's **Vera Rubin AI rack-scale solutions** are now ramping into full production. Looking ahead, NVIDIA holds its **Annual Meeting of Stockholders on Wednesday 24 June**. Analysts are broadly bullish — 62 analysts rate it a Strong Buy with an average 12-month price target of $298.93, representing roughly 42% upside from current levels. Watch for any shareholder meeting announcements mid-week.

**SPDR Gold MiniShares (GLDM) — $83.01–$84.61 range**
Gold has been under pressure. The precious metal fell to around **$4,150 per ounce** on Friday — its lowest since 11 June — and is on track for a third consecutive weekly decline. The culprit is a **stronger US dollar** and the **Federal Reserve's more hawkish tone** following last week's policy meeting. With nine of eighteen Fed policymakers now signalling at least one rate hike by year-end, demand for non-yielding gold has softened. Goldman Sachs has also **lowered its year-end gold price forecast to $4,900 from $5,400**. Headwinds look likely to continue near-term.

**Alphabet (GOOG)**
No major company-specific news found for Alphabet today. The broader technology sector recovery last week (Nasdaq up 2.43%) would have been supportive. Monitor for any developments tied to antitrust proceedings or artificial intelligence product announcements.

**Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing (TSM)**
Taiwan Semiconductor is in the spotlight after it emerged that **Apple has secured more than 50% of TSMC's 2-nanometer production capacity for 2026**, earmarked for the A20 chip in the iPhone 18 and the M6 processors in MacBooks. TSMC's aggressive capital expenditure plan of **$52–56 billion** for 2026 underscores its dominance — the company controls approximately **72% of the global chip market**. AI-driven demand is expected to sustain strong growth.

On the positive side, **Wolfe Research upgraded the stock to Hold from Sell** last week, citing Palantir's strong positioning in government and enterprise artificial intelligence contracts. The stock traded between $125.01 and $131.45 on Friday. No major catalyst expected today.

**Apple (AAPL)**

Apple and Qualcomm are also said to be **co-developing AI-driven 2-nanometer chips** targeted for late 2026 product cycles. No specific price-moving event today.

However, there was a positive development on 15 June when the stock **jumped 4.6%** after reports of a peace deal reopening the **Strait of Hormuz** caused Treasury yields to fall — software companies are particularly sensitive to long-term interest rates, and the drop in the 10-year yield to 4.41% boosted valuations. On the strategic side, Qualcomm is **expanding into artificial intelligence and data centre chips**, including a $2.3 billion data centre acquisition and plans to ship custom data centre chips to a major cloud provider in 2026. Notably, NVIDIA Chief Executive Jensen Huang publicly stated that **Marvell could become a trillion-dollar company**, lending considerable weight to its growth narrative. ## Section 5 — Broader US Market Events

*Here's the bigger picture for the US stock market this week.*

**Markets reopen after a long weekend.** US exchanges were closed on Thursday 19 June for the **Juneteenth national holiday**, so today — Monday 22 June — marks the return of a full trading week. Expect liquidity to reset and volumes to pick up.

**CoreWeave and four other AI names join the Nasdaq-100 today.** Effective before today's market open, **CoreWeave (ticker: CRWV)** — the AI cloud infrastructure provider — joins the Nasdaq-100 Index, just fifteen months after its initial public offering. It is joined by **Astera Labs, Nebius Group, Rocket Lab, and Teradyne** as part of the June quarterly rebalance. Index inclusion forces all passive funds tracking the Nasdaq-100 to mechanically buy these stocks, which is expected to generate significant demand. CoreWeave was already up around 5% in pre-market trading after the announcement.

**The Federal Reserve sent a hawkish signal.** At last week's meeting — the first chaired by new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh — policymakers **held the benchmark interest rate steady at 3.5% to 3.75%**, but raised their inflation forecasts and removed a previously projected 2026 rate cut from the policy projections. Nine of the eighteen participating policymakers now expect **at least one rate hike by the end of 2026**. This is weighing on growth and technology stocks, and is a headwind for gold.

**Key economic data due this week.** Wall Street is watching closely for a **major inflation update on Thursday**, along with end-of-month readings on **first-quarter Gross Domestic Product** and **May Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) prices**. These could move markets significantly depending on whether they support or challenge the Fed's hawkish stance.

**Last week's market performance:** The Nasdaq Composite rose 2.43%, the S&P 500 gained 0.93%, and the Dow Jones Industrial Average advanced 0.71%, driven largely by the technology sector and NVIDIA's RTX Spark announcement.

**Oil prices remain elevated.** West Texas Intermediate crude oil was last seen at approximately $92.54 per barrel, with geopolitical tensions having eased somewhat following the reported Strait of Hormuz peace deal — but energy prices remain well above historical norms.
